3.1 Blood

Blood consists of a protein-rich fluid known as plasma, in which are suspended cellular elements: white blood cells, red blood cells, and platelets. 8% of the body weight (5600mL in a 70kg man) 1

55% of volume is plasma, 45% of volume is cellular components. Serum = Plasma – clotting factors *Blood cell production : yolk sac  liver  spleen, lymph nodes  bone marrow

*Pluripotent Hematopoietic stem cells (PHSCs) are bone marrow cells that are capable of producing all types of blood cells. Blood cell Cell size Erythrocyte 6.5 ~ 8 μm Leukocyte
Neutrophil, Eosinophil, Basophil 12 ~ 15 μm Lymphocyte 6 ~ 18 μm Monocyte 12 ~ 20 μm Platelet 2 ~ 4 μm

Blood viscosity Viscosity (mPa/s) = (shear stress (mPa))/(shear rate (s)) Viscosity, the reciprocal of fluidity, is the frictional blow resistance between layers that must be overcome by a driving force to cause shearing and hence flow. Blood is defined as a non-Newtonian fluid. Its non-Newtonian behavior is principally due to red cell aggregation at low shear rates and red cell deformation at high shear rates. Relationship between shear rate and viscosity in a Newtonian fluid and blood.

*Mechanism of shear rate dependence of blood viscosity.

The two most important parameters that influence the blood viscosity profile are hematocrit and plasma proteins (fibrinogen); lipid profiles such as LDL-C, HDL-C and triglyceride also affect low-shear blood viscosity more poignantly than high-shear viscosity.
